About

The purpose of this website is to explore the intersectionality of how race and class affects LGBTQ+ and gender-nonconforming people within their workplace. Focusing on the hiring rates, compensation, and workplace experiences, I want to highlight how this demographic is disproportionally affected as compared to their white, heterosexual, and cisgender peers. Motivations Behind This Resource

As a gender-nonconforming person, I am acutely aware of my identity and often feel compelled to conceal this facet of myself at work. My fears of jeopardizing career opportunities are compounded by the need to navigate workplace dynamics carefully, often placating colleagues to avoid conflict. Additionally, as a person of color, I am deeply interested in exploring how the intersectionality of race, class, and gender identity further shapes the experiences of LGBTQ+ and gender-nonconforming individuals in the workforce. Through this project, I aim to shed light on the various forms of discrimination faced by LGBTQ+ and gender-nonconforming people in the workplace. While landmark rulings such as Bostock v. Clayton County offer some legal protection, more subtle and systemic forms of bias persist and is not covered by legislation. By presenting this information, I hope to empower my peers to approach the job market with a better understanding of their rights, strategies for self-advocacy, and tools to navigate these challenges. I also want to examine how workplace discrimination affects other aspects of life for LGBTQ+ and gender-nonconforming individuals, including mental health, financial stability, and social relationships. This broader perspective will help contextualize the struggles faced by our community and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the societal barriers we encounter. My ultimate goal is to not only inform but also inspire action against unjust treatment in professional environments, fostering workplaces that are truly inclusive and equitable for all.
